Hi, I’m Dan.

An experienced design leader who sincerely believes design can make a positive difference in people’s lives.
I am passionate about working with good people to find simple solutions to complex problems.

I am the Head of Design and User Experience at Crowdpac, a political crowdfunding and engagement platform. I plan, research, design, build, and test tools that give citizens a new way to participate in the political process. We have helped hundreds of people run for office for the first time, and helped candidates from Library Board to City Council to President raise millions of dollars and connect with their community of supporters.

As a freelance designer I've worked with great organizations like Change.org, Trunk Club, OpenGovFoundation, Hillary for America, Precision Strategies, Northwestern University, Planned Parenthood, and many more.

Prior to that I was fortunate enough to have the experience of a lifetime, as Lead Interactive Designer for Barack Obama’s 2012 re-election campaign. I designed social organizing platforms, fundraising tools, and data visualizations that were used by millions of people and raised millions of dollars. I was then named Design Director while the organization transitioned from a campaign into Organizing for Action.
